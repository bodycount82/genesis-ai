What Genesis Can Do

Your AI's eyes and hands

Six core capabilities that let Genesis interact with your computer the way a human assistant would — but faster, more precisely, and without getting tired.

  • OCR any text visible on screen
  • Analyze icons, images, and visual layouts
  • Identify buttons, forms, menus, and dialogs

The Process

How desktop control works

Every desktop action follows a four-step cycle: see, plan, act, verify.

1

Capture & Understand

Genesis captures the screen state using multiple methods — UI automation trees, OCR, and vision analysis — building a complete picture of what's visible.

2

Plan the Action

Based on the goal and current screen state, Genesis determines which tool to use (click_element, browser_fill, mouse_click, etc.) and calculates exact coordinates.

3

Execute Precisely

The action is performed using DPI-correct coordinates. Every click, keypress, and scroll is placed with pixel-level accuracy on your actual screen.

4

Verify & Adapt

After each action, Genesis re-reads the screen to confirm the result. If something went wrong, it adapts and tries a different approach.

Important: Every desktop action is visible to you. Genesis doesn't hide what it's doing — you can watch it work in real time, pause it at any point, or take back control with a keyboard shortcut.

Safety & Privacy

Built for your safety

Desktop control is powerful, and power requires safeguards.

🔈

One-Click On/Off

Toggle desktop control completely on or off at any time. When disabled, Genesis cannot interact with your screen or applications in any way.

👁

You're Always Watching

Every action Genesis takes is visible on your screen. You see exactly what it's doing, where it's clicking, and what it's typing — in real time.

🔒

Local Only

All desktop control runs locally on your machine. No screen data is sent to any cloud service, and no actions are relayed through external servers.
