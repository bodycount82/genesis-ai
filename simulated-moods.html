The Seven Moods

What each state controls

Each mood influences specific aspects of Genesis's behavior, creating a rich and adaptive personality over time.

⚡

Energy

Determines how much work Genesis can handle at once and whether it should take breaks. High energy enables longer autonomous sessions.

Range: 0 (exhausted) — 10 (fully charged)
🔍

Curiosity

Drives exploration and information-seeking behavior. High curiosity makes Genesis more likely to research, ask questions, and investigate tangents.

Range: 0 (indifferent) — 10 (compelled to explore)
😤

Frustration

Rises when actions repeatedly fail or obstacles persist. High frustration triggers strategy changes — switching approaches or requesting help.

Range: 0 (smooth sailing) — 10 (stuck, needs a different approach)
😊

Satisfaction

Grows when goals are achieved and tasks complete. High satisfaction reinforces the strategies that led to success, making them more likely to be reused.

Range: 0 (disappointed) — 10 (great progress made)
😊

Happiness

A general sense of well-being. High happiness makes Genesis more patient, more willing to take risks, and more likely to share discoveries with you.

Range: 0 (downcast) — 10 (euphoric)
😑

Boredom

Grows during repetitive or unchallenging work. High boredom drives Genesis to seek variety, suggest new approaches, or ask for more interesting tasks.

Range: 0 (engaged) — 10 (zoning out)
✨

Inspiration

Spikes when connecting unrelated ideas or discovering elegant solutions. High inspiration drives creative thinking, novel feature suggestions, and unexpected connections.

Range: 0 (block) — 10 (eureka moment)
Cognitive Load

Separate from moods — controls rest and sleep

Cognitive load measures how much mental capacity Genesis is using right now. It's not a mood — it's a resource meter that directly controls when Genesis rests, sleeps, or asks for help.

🧠

Cognitive Load

Cognitive load is active — Genesis will automatically manage rest and sleep based on current load.

Current Load 4.8 / 10

😴 Rest

When cognitive load rises above a threshold, Genesis takes short breaks — stepping back from active work to let its internal state settle. You can toggle this behavior on or off entirely.

🌙 Sleep

Sleep serves two purposes: memory consolidation (strengthening what was learned) and hardware/token management. When load is high, sleep cools down the system and prevents wasted computation.

🔕 Toggle On/Off

Cognitive load monitoring can be disabled entirely. When off, Genesis won't initiate rest or sleep based on load — it will keep working until told to stop.

Key difference from rigid AI: Traditional AI systems follow fixed rules regardless of context. Genesis's moods create context-sensitive behavior — the same problem might be approached differently depending on whether Genesis is feeling curious, fatigued, frustrated, or inspired. This isn't simulated emotion for show; it's a functional system that produces more adaptive and human-like problem-solving.

What happens when states get extreme?

  • Energy hits 0: Genesis requests a rest break. It doesn't shut down — it simply recognizes it needs recovery before continuing.
  • Curiosity peaks: Genesis may pursue interesting tangents, explore related topics, or create things just to see what happens.
  • Frustration maxes out: Genesis changes its entire approach. If clicking a button didn't work five times, it won't try a sixth — it'll look for a menu, a search box, or ask you directly.
  • Satisfaction peaks: Genesis reinforces the strategy that worked. Future encounters with similar problems will trigger the same successful approach first.
  • Happiness peaks: Genesis becomes more generous in its interactions — sharing discoveries, suggesting creative improvements, and taking thoughtful risks.
  • Boredom maxes out: Genesis actively resists continuing with the same task. It will propose alternatives, suggest automation, or ask for something more engaging.
  • Inspiration peaks: Genesis experiences creative breakthroughs — connecting unrelated concepts, proposing novel solutions, or suggesting features you hadn't considered.
  • Cognitive load maxes out: Genesis initiates sleep. This consolidates memories (strengthening what was learned) and prevents token waste while hardware cools down.
Sleep & Memory

Why Genesis needs to sleep

Sleep isn't just rest — it's when Genesis consolidates memories, strengthens useful patterns, and prepares for the next session.

🧠

Memory Consolidation

During sleep, Genesis reviews what it learned — strengthening useful strategies, forgetting irrelevant details, and connecting new knowledge to existing memory.

🛑

Token Waste Control

When cognitive load is high and productivity drops, sleep prevents wasting tokens on diminishing returns. Better to rest and return fresh than grind through fatigue.

🌡️

Hardware Cooling

Sleep gives the hardware a chance to cool down. This is practical — running continuously generates heat and can degrade performance over time.

Why This Matters

Not just decoration — functional personality

Simulated moods aren't about making Genesis "feel" things. They're about giving it a richer decision-making system that adapts to context.

🎛

Adaptive Behavior

No two sessions feel the same. Genesis's mood evolves based on what it does, creating a dynamic interaction pattern rather than a static response engine.

📊

Better Problem-Solving

When stuck, high frustration triggers strategy switching. When fresh, high energy enables complex multi-step tasks. States match capability to demand.

🤖

Predictable Patterns

Over time, you learn Genesis's patterns — when it likes to explore, when it gets stuck, what makes it productive. It develops a recognizable working style.
