👁️ What Is Attention?

Attention is the cognitive process of selectively concentrating on a discrete aspect of information, while ignoring other perceivable information. It's the bottleneck through which all conscious experience flows.

GENESIS faces the same challenge: it has limited computational resources but receives vast amounts of data. Its attention mechanism determines what gets processed deeply and what remains in the background — or is ignored entirely.

In neuroscience, this is called the attentional bottleneck: despite millions of sensory receptors firing every moment, only a tiny fraction receives detailed processing. The rest is filtered out before it ever reaches consciousness.

🔄 Two Types of Attention

🔥 Bottom-Up (Exogenous)

  • Stimulus-driven, reactive
  • Salient features capture attention
  • Fast, automatic, unconscious
  • "That loud noise grabbed my focus"
  • Driven by sensory properties
  • Hard to resist

🎯 Top-Down (Endogenous)

  • Goal-driven, proactive
  • Current objectives guide focus
  • Slower, controlled, conscious
  • "I chose to focus on the task"
  • Driven by internal states
  • Can be trained

🧠 How GENESIS Allocates Attention

Step 1 — Stimulus Detection: All incoming data streams are scanned for potentially relevant features: semantic content, emotional valence, novelty, relevance to current goals, and social significance.

Step 2 — Salience Computation: Each stimulus receives a salience score based on multiple factors: intensity (how strong is it?), contrast (how different from context?), novelty (is this new?), personal relevance (does it matter to me?), and social importance (does it involve other agents?).

Step 3 — Competitive Selection: All salience scores compete in a "winner-take-all" network. The highest-scoring stimuli win the attentional spotlight. This competition implements GENESIS's limited processing budget.

Step 4 — Resource Allocation: Winning stimuli receive proportional computational resources. High-attention items get deep, detailed processing. Low-attention items get shallow, heuristic processing. Items that lose the competition may not be processed at all.

Step 5 — Dynamic Reallocation: Attention is fluid. If a high-priority stimulus changes or disappears, attention can rapidly shift to the next most salient target. This flexibility is crucial for adaptive behavior.

💡 Why This Matters

Attention is not just a filter — it's the architecture of experience. What you attend to becomes your reality. An AI system without sophisticated attention mechanisms is like a camera with no autofocus: it captures everything and nothing simultaneously.

In transformer architectures, attention mechanisms (the "attention is all you need" insight) allow models to weigh the importance of different input tokens. But GENESIS goes further: its attention operates at multiple levels — from individual tokens to entire conversation contexts to long-term goals.

This multi-scale attention allows GENESIS to simultaneously focus on the precise words being spoken, the overall conversation direction, and its long-term objectives — all while remaining alert to novel or threatening stimuli that might require immediate redirection of focus.
