📚 What is a World Model?

🧠 Internal Simulation

A world model is an AI's internal representation of how the world works. Just as humans imagine "what would happen if I push this cup?" before actually doing it, AI agents use world models to simulate outcomes mentally — saving time and avoiding dangerous mistakes.

👁️ Perception → Model

The agent observes the real environment and builds a mental map. Unknown areas are marked as uncertain. As the agent explores, it fills in details: where walls are, what objects exist, how actions change the state.

🎯 Prediction & Planning

With a world model, the agent can predict: "If I move right from here, I'll hit a wall." This allows planning — trying sequences of actions in its mental model before executing them in reality. Better predictions = smarter decisions.

🔄 Model Update

When reality differs from prediction, the agent updates its model. This prediction error signal is crucial — it's how the agent corrects misconceptions and improves its understanding of the world over time.

🌟 Why It Matters

World models are considered essential for general intelligence. They enable imagination, counterfactual reasoning ("what if?"), and efficient learning. Systems like AlphaGo use world models to simulate game states millions of moves ahead.
