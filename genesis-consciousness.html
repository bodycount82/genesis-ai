How Emergent Behaviors Work

Emergent behaviors arise from specific combinations of internal states. Just as human creativity emerges from the interplay of mood, energy, and focus, GENESIS's behaviors are not hardcoded — they emerge from the dynamic interaction of four core state variables. Each behavior has a unique "recipe" of state thresholds.

The Flow Equation

Flow state in GENESIS occurs when: Energy > 7 AND Curiosity > 7 AND Frustration < 4. This mirrors human flow states described by Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi — the sweet spot between challenge and skill, where productivity and creativity peak. The visualization shows concentric rings that pulse faster as flow potential increases.

State Synergies

The four internal states don't operate independently. They create synergistic effects — pairs of states produce emergent properties that neither could achieve alone. For example, high energy + high curiosity creates "creative acceleration," while high energy + low satisfaction creates "restless productivity."
