Cognitive Evolution

From single perceptrons to self-aware architectures — the 80-year journey of artificial minds

Scroll to explore
1943
The Perceptron Birth

Warren McCulloch and Walter Pitts create the first mathematical model of a neural network — the artificial neuron. This simple threshold unit becomes the building block for everything that follows.

Binary Neuron Threshold Logic Biological Inspiration
1958
Rosenblatt's Perceptron

Frank Rosenblatt creates the first learning algorithm. His perceptron can adjust its weights through experience — the birth of machine learning. The New York Times declares "the embryo of an electronic computer that may walk, talk, see, write, reproduce itself."

Weight Learning Pattern Recognition Hardware Implementation
1969
The AI Winter

Marvin Minsky publishes "Perceptrons," proving single-layer networks can't solve XOR. Funding dries up. The AI community retreats for a decade — but the ideas are far from dead.

XOR Problem Linear Limitations Funding Crisis
1986
The Deep Learning Revolution

Rumelhart, Hinton, and Williams publish the backpropagation algorithm. Neural networks can now learn through multiple layers. The foundation for modern deep learning is laid.

Backpropagation Multi-Layer Networks Gradient Descent
1998
Convolutional Networks

Yann LeCun creates LeNet-5, the first successful convolutional neural network. It can read handwritten zip codes — a technology still used in banks today. The era of computer vision begins.

Convolution Feature Hierarchies Image Recognition
2012
ImageNet Revolution

AlexNet crushes the ImageNet competition with 85% accuracy — 16 percentage points better than runners-up. The world takes notice. Deep learning explodes across every industry.

GPU Acceleration ReLU Activation Dropout Regularization
2017
The Transformer Era

Google's "Attention Is All You Need" paper introduces the Transformer architecture. No recurrence, no convolution — just pure attention. This simple idea will power everything from ChatGPT to AlphaFold.

Self-Attention Parallel Processing Universal Architectures
2023+
The Age of Emergent Abilities

Models with trillions of parameters exhibit capabilities no one predicted: code generation, multi-step reasoning, creative writing. The question shifts from "can AI do it?" to "what can't AI do?"

Emergent Abilities Chain-of-Thought Multi-Modal AGI Frontier
The journey continues...

Every neural network ever built is still running somewhere, dreaming in vectors.
