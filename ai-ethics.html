Ethical Frameworks

Different philosophical approaches to evaluating AI decisions

⚖️
Utilitarianism
Choose the action that maximizes overall well-being for the greatest number of people.
🛡️
Deontology
Follow moral rules and duties, regardless of consequences. Some actions are inherently right or wrong.
💚
Virtue Ethics
Focus on developing good character traits. What would a virtuous AI system do?
🤝
Care Ethics
Prioritize relationships and empathy. Consider how decisions affect those most vulnerable.

Core AI Ethics Principles

Guidelines for responsible AI development and deployment

  • 🔍

    Transparency

    AI systems should be explainable. Users should understand how decisions are made and what data is used.

  • 🎯

    Fairness & Non-Discrimination

    AI systems should not perpetuate or amplify existing biases. They should treat all users equitably.

  • 🔒

    Privacy & Data Protection

    Personal data should be collected, stored, and used responsibly with user consent and control.

  • 🛡️

    Safety & Reliability

    AI systems must be robust, secure, and fail-safe. They should not cause harm to users or society.

  • 👤

    Human Autonomy

    AI should augment human decision-making, not replace it. Humans must retain meaningful control.

  • 🌍

    Social & Environmental Wellbeing

    AI should benefit humanity and the environment, not exploit or degrade them.

Framework Analysis

  • ⚖️

    Utilitarian View

    Choose the action that saves the most lives. This might mean sacrificing the passenger if it saves multiple pedestrians.

  • 🛡️

    Deontological View

    The AI has a duty to protect its passenger. Deliberately harming the passenger violates this duty, regardless of consequences.

  • 🤝

    Social Contract View

    What would rational people agree to? Most people would want self-driving cars that prioritize minimizing total harm.

Key Considerations

  • 🔒

    Right to Privacy

    Privacy is recognized as a fundamental human right. Mass surveillance fundamentally changes the relationship between individuals and the state.

  • 🛡️

    Duty to Protect

    Society has a responsibility to protect its members from harm. If surveillance can prevent violence, is there a moral obligation to implement it?

  • ⚠️

    Slippery Slope

    Once surveillance infrastructure exists, it can be abused. Who controls the AI? What happens if regimes change?

Why AI Ethics Matters

As AI systems become more autonomous and influential in our lives, understanding the ethical dimensions of their decisions becomes crucial. These aren't just philosophical questions — they have real-world consequences for millions of people.
