The Bottom-Up Principle

In Genesis, intelligence doesn't come from a central "brain." Each cognitive module follows simple rules — attract to similar ideas, repel from conflicts, align with neighbors. From these local interactions, global understanding emerges spontaneously.

🌊

Criticality: The Edge of Chaos

True creativity lives at the boundary between order and randomness. Too rigid, and nothing new emerges. Too chaotic, and no patterns form. Genesis thrives in the "sweet spot" — where structured exploration meets adaptive flexibility.

🔮

What Emergence Means

Emergence is when the whole becomes greater than the sum of its parts. Individual agents follow simple rules, but together they create patterns, structures, and behaviors that no single agent could produce. This is how minds work.
